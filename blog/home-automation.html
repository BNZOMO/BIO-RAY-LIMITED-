Smart Home Security 101

How to combine smart cameras, locks, lighting and alarms so you can see and control your home from anywhere.

Good to know: You don’t have to make your whole home “smart” at once. In most Kenyan homes we start with one or two priority areas, then expand over time.

1Smart CCTV systems

Modern cameras do more than record to a hard drive. When installed correctly, they act as both a deterrent and an investigation tool.

  • Choose cameras with night vision and good low‑light performance.
  • Enable motion alerts only on key zones (gate, doors, parking) to avoid constant notifications.
  • Use cloud backup or remote access so you can still view footage even if the recorder is damaged or stolen.

2Smart door locks & access

What they can do

Open via PIN, card, fingerprint or phone; create temporary visitor codes; see who entered and when.

Where they work best

Main doors, apartment doors, and office entrances where many people share access.

For rentals, we usually recommend non‑destructive smart locks that can be removed when you move out.

3Smart lighting & motion sensors

Lighting is one of the cheapest upgrades with the biggest impact on security.

  • Use motion‑activated lights near gates, parking and corridors.
  • Set simple “away” scenes so some lights turn on automatically in the evening when you travel.
  • Combine sensors with cameras: when motion is detected, the nearest camera can start recording at higher quality.

4Smart alarms & sensors

Instead of one big alarm panel hidden in a cupboard, small wireless sensors can be placed on doors, windows and key areas.

  • Receive alerts on your phone when doors or windows are opened unexpectedly.
  • Arm different “modes” (night mode, away mode) depending on who is at home.
  • Integrate with sirens, lights and CCTV for a coordinated response.

5Mobile app & ecosystem

To keep things simple, we prefer building around one main app that controls most of your devices instead of five separate apps.

  • Pick devices that support the same platform (e.g. Tuya, Google, or the brand’s own ecosystem).
  • Ensure the app supports multiple users so family members or guards can have their own logins.
  • Ask about data usage — especially if your home internet has a limited bundle.
Example project: 3‑bedroom home in Nairobi
Start with: 3–4 smart cameras (gate, back, corridor, sitting room) + smart lock on the main door + motion‑sensor floodlight at the gate. Later, add smart plugs and scenes for lights and TV.
